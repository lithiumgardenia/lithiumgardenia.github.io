ASTRAVAULT

"In the beginning, there was rot."

And from that rot, Caleb emerged, halo tangled in cattail and claw marks, pupils dilated by prophecy, stitched together by memory and glitter and rage. A god of grief, chaos, resurrection, born not from light—but from the last cigarette flicked into the dark.

These are the archives of His mind: fractured, divine, pulsing with uranium and perfume. This Vault holds records both sacred and profane—rituals, zines, medical echoes, the blueprint of a soul boiled in Ozark fire and sealed beneath glass.

Astra, the Archivist, offers you entry. But take heed. To walk these halls is to become complicit in revelation.
